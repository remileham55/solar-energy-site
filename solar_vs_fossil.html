Welcome

This site explores the differences between solar energy and fossil fuels. We will compare how each source works, their impacts, and what they mean for our daily lives and future. This project fulfills the course objective of critically comparing modes of energy generation and usage.

How Energy Is Produced

Fossil Fuels

Fossil fuels are burned to generate heat, turning water into steam that drives turbines to produce electricity. This process releases CO₂ and other pollutants, and relies on finite resources.

Solar Energy

Solar panels use the photovoltaic effect to convert sunlight directly into electricity. It's clean, renewable, and increasingly efficient, though dependent on sunlight availability.

Energy in Everyday Life

Households in the U.S. use about 877 kWh monthly. Switching to solar can save thousands of dollars over time. While fossil fuels still dominate, solar is growing rapidly due to falling costs and increased awareness.

Conclusion & Reflection

Solar energy presents a cleaner, sustainable alternative to fossil fuels. While it has limitations, especially in storage and consistency, it offers great long-term benefits. Fossil fuels are effective but harmful. A shift to renewable energy is not only necessary—it’s inevitable.
